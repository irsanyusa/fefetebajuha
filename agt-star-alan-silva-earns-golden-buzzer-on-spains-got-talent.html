Alan Silva, who finished America’s Got Talent Season 15 in fifth place, has taken his aerial routine to Spain’s Got Talent. Silva’s audition was so impressive, it earned the Golden Buzzer from one of the judges on the show.

Alan Silva Receives Golden Buzzer on Spain’s Got Talent

Silva performed a mesmerizing aerial silks routine in which he showed off some acrobatic moves and at one point even seemed to fly around the stage. He finished the audition with a dangerous-looking drop from the ceiling.

Afterward, the crowd started chanting for the judges to press the Golden Buzzer for Silva’s performance. Most of the judges also responded positively to the audition. Unfortunately, judge Risto Mejide wasn’t as impressed. He earned boos from the crowd for his critique.

Luckily, Silva didn’t need Mejide’s approval to move on in the competition, because fellow judge Dani Martínez hit the Golden Buzzer with his head. Silva got emotional as the gold confetti rained down around him.

The show’s host, Santi Millán, stepped out on stage to show Silva a video chat with his wife and son. He told them he received the Golden Buzzer in a sweet moment.

This Is Silva’s First Golden Buzzer

Although he made it all the way to fifth place on AGT Season 15, Silva did not receive the Golden Buzzer during his time on the show. That makes his recent Golden Buzzer on Spain’s Got Talent even more special.

Silva comes from a circus family. His brother, Alfredo Silva, is part of the AGT danger act Deadly Games. Silva shared in his audition for AGT that people told him he should be a clown because of his size, adding that he’s “been bullied my entire life.”

The aerialist continues to prove his bullies wrong. We can’t wait to see how far Silva goes on Spain’s Got Talent this season.
