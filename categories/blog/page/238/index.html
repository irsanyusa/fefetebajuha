U.S. Jobs &amp; Labor Report: March 2023

published on June 18, 2024
A year into the Federal Reserve’s aggressive rate hiking cycle, there are still few signs of weakness in the U.S. labor market. Job growth is strong, unemployment is extremely low and few Americans are reporting lost pay or income. However, recession risks remain. Rapid interest rate hikes have historically been successful at putting the brakes on an overheated economy, but it takes time for this to occur as higher borrowing costs make their way through the economy, cooling investment and consumption.
