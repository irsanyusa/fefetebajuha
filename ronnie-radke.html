Ronnie Radke (Rocker) - On This Day

Full Name: Ronald Joseph Radke
Profession: Rocker

Biography: Ronnie Radke is an American musician who is currently the lead singer of rock band Falling in Reverse. He was previously the lead singer of the post-hardcore band Escape the Fate.

Radke had a difficult childhood due to his mother's drug abuse and absence. He taught himself to play guitar and piano at a young age, later forming several bands in high school.

In 2004, Radke co-founded Escape the Fate, which became popular after being discovered by My Chemical Romance. They released their debut album "Dying Is Your Latest Fashion" in 2006.

In 2008, Radke was sentenced to 2.5 years in prison for violating probation related to a 2006 altercation, resulting in his departure from Escape the Fate.

While incarcerated, he formed Falling in Reverse and wrote their first songs. After his release in 2010, the band released their debut album "The Drug in Me Is You" (2011).

Radke had a public feud with Craig Mabbitt, his replacement in Escape the Fate, which was resolved when the two bands co-headlined the "Bury the Hatchet" tour in 2014.

From 2012 to 2015, Radke faced several legal issues, including arrests for domestic violence and assault at a concert. He has also been vocal about denouncing religion's poor treatment of LGBTQ people.

Falling in Reverse has released four studio albums, with their 2020 single "Popular Monster" topping the rock charts. Radke also released a rap mixtape, "Watch Me," in 2014, featuring collaborations with other artists.

In 2022, he published a memoir titled "I Can Explain," reflecting on his life and career. Concerning the title, he explained: "I named it that 'cause it’s really funny to me 'cause I’ve gotten in so much trouble and have a bad reputation."
