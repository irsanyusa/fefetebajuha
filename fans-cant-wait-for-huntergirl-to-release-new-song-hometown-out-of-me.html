American Idol Season 20 runner-up HunterGirl recently teased more of a new original song called “Hometown Out of Me,” and fans can’t wait for her to release the complete version. The track pays tribute to where she came from.

HunterGirl Teases More of Song “Hometown Out of Me”

Last month, HunterGirl shared on Instagram that she had just written a new song dedicated to her hometown of Winchester, Tennessee. She said she wrote the track, called “Hometown Out of Me,” with Jimmy Robbins and Laura Veltz.

HunterGirl sat in her car and played the song’s chorus. Then, over the weekend, she shared another car video, this time playing the song’s first verse, along with the chorus again. As she wrote in the caption, “This one’s for my hometown. Thank you guys for always supporting my dreams. Love y’all so much!”

“Mama kinda had a feeling / Thank God she always got me,” HunterGirl sings on the track. “A county line and a ceiling / Wasn’t ever gonna stop me / Wings are made for open air / And there’s a great big world out there / There ain’t a map dot I don’t wanna see / But this one is a part of me.”

Fans Are Loving the New Song

HunterGirl finished the video by asking fans to let her know what they think of the song. Commenters shared their love for the track and excitement for the singer to finally release the full version for everyone to enjoy.

“GO HUNTER GO!!” wrote third place finisher Leah Marlene. Meanwhile, Season 19 runner-up Willie Spence called the song “amazing,” adding fire and heart emojis.

When asked when fans can expect “Hometown Out of Me” to be released, HunterGirl responded, “I hope I get to put it out soon.” In the meantime, the singer already has an EP out called One Day, as well as her Idol single “Red Bird.”
